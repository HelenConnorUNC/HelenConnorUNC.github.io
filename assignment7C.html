Last of the West
Grand Teton National Park boasts over 310,000 acres rich with extrodinary wildlife, pristine lakes, and alpine terrain.

Located just noth of Jackson, Wyoming, the Grand Tetons hold a diverse and longstanding history within its natural beauty.
What to Explore
JENNY Lake
Carved by glaciers over thousands of years, the crystal-clear waters of Jenny Lake reflect the grandeur of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can explore its shores via hiking trails, take a scenic boat ride, or simply bask in the breathtaking views from the various viewpoints along the lake. Hidden within a wilderness of towering pines, Jenny Lake provides a tranquil escape for all its admirers. The iconic Cascade Canyon trailhead begins at Jenny Lake, inviting adventurers to delve deeper into the heart of Grand Teton National Park's wilderness. The lake is not just a destination; it's a gateway to the rugged and majestic landscapes that define the Teton region.
Oxbow Bend
Renowned for its breathtaking scenery, this bend in the Snake River offers a stunning reflection of the Teton Range on its calm waters. The serene landscape is adorned with lush vegetation and framed by majestic mountains, creating a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers alike. Oxbow Bend is a popular spot for observing diverse fauna, including moose, elk, and a variety of bird species. Whether visitors come to marvel at the sunrise illuminating the peaks or to witness the tranquility of the mirrored waters, Oxbow Bend stands as a captivating testament to the natural beauty that defines the American West.
Mormon Row
Set against the dramatic backdrop of the Teton Range, these weathered barns and rustic homes tell the story of the Mormon pioneers who settled in this area in the 1900s. The most iconic structure, known as the Moulton Barn, has become an enduring symbol of the American West and a favorite subject for photographers seeking to capture the essence of rural life in the shadow of the Tetons. Mormon Row offers visitors a glimpse into the resilience and resourcefulness of the early settlers who faced the challenges of the frontier, while also providing a stunning panorama of the Teton peaks, making it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.
History of the Park
The stunning beauty and abundant wildlife of the valley have been drawing in humans for over 11,000 years. People first ventured into the area as the glaciers receeded. By the times the first Europeans arraived, tribes such as the Shoshone, Bannock, Blackfoot, Crow, Flathead, Gros Ventre, Nez Perce and others were occupying the valley and harvesting its seasonal riches.

European-American exploration began in the early 19th century, with fur trappers and explorers like John Colter venturing into the Teton Range. The Lewis and Clark Expedition, in 1807-1808, provided some of the first detailed descriptions of the region.

In 1929, President Calvin Coolidge designated the area as Jackson Hole National Monument, preserving it for future generations. In 1950, the monument was folded into Grand Teton National Park, officially established to protect the spectacular Teton Range and its surrounding ecosystem.
Experience all that
Wyoming has to offer
Jackson Hole offers a wealth of activities for every visitor. Enjoy endless outdoor recreation, renowned shopping, dining and night life, and iconic events, like the Jackson Hole Rodeo, the Nightly Shootout at the Town Square, the Grand Teton Music Festival, the Teton County Fair, Old West Days and the Fall Arts Festival. Here, the Spirit of the Old West is alive and vibrant.
Saddle Up
Saddle up and explore the park on horseback. Riding on horseback allows you to connect with the natural beauty of the park in a way that is both imersive and timeless.

The Jackson Hole Rodeo exhibits the true spirit of the West with calf roping, barrel racing, bull riding, and kids’ events. You’ll find all this and more every Wednesday, Friday and Saturday night from Memorial Day through Labor Day weekend. Dust off your boots, grab a cold one and experience a tradition as old as the West itself.
Town Square
Known for its unique blend of rustic charm and vibrant energy, the Town Square is a focal point for locals and visitors alike.

The square offers a blend of rustic charm and entertainment, featuring live music and the Old West-inspired Town Square Shootout. Serving as a gateway to the natural wonders of Jackson Hole, it provides a lively focal point for both locals and visitors, capturing the essence of the American West.
