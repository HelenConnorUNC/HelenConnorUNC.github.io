Cowboy Up
The Jackson Hole Rodeo exhibits the true spirit of the West with calf roping, barrel racing, bull riding, and kids’ events. You’ll find all this and more every Wednesday, Friday and Saturday night from Memorial Day through Labor Day weekend. Dust off your boots, grab a cold one and experience a tradition as old as the West itself.
Town Square
The “Town Square” was once where ranchers came to stock up on supplies and hear the latest news from around the valley at the Million Dollar Cowboy Bar. And it still is.

Today, the Town Square is home to local shops, dining and nightlife. After a day in the mountains, unwind with a meal at any one of the valley's celebrated restaurants and belly up at the Silver Dollar or Cowboy Bar, where local folklore flows as easy as the local brews.

What to Explore
JENNY Lake
A glacial lake at the foot of the Grand Teton, Jenny Lake and the surrounding sagebrush flats attract all kinds of animals, from elk and moose to grizzly and black bears. Hiking trails offer access into the vast Teton wilderness, including Inspiration Point, which provides stunning valley-wide views.
oxbow bend
Tucked just inside the entrance to Grand Teton National Park, this meandering offshoot of the Snake River is home to a surprising diversity of wildlife. A bird watcher's paradise, the stretch of water is frequented by bald eagles, osprey and trumpeter swans — not to mention, beavers, otters, bears and moose.
Mormon Row
The historic Mormon Row on Antelope Flats was one of the first settlements in Jackson Hole. Today, visitors can expect to see bison, antelope, coyotes, and birds of prey alongside the iconic structures, which are the most photographed barns in the world.
